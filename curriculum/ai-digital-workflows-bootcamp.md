# AI & Digital Workflows — Bootcamp Curriculum

## The Pitch

**"Become the AI expert at your company, classroom, or organization — in 2 weekends."**

A hands-on, portfolio-producing bootcamp for college students and early-career professionals (18-25) in Armenian, Moldovan, and Georgian communities. No coding required. Students leave with real AI workflows they can use Monday morning and the confidence to be the person everyone asks for help.

---

## Why This, Why Now, Why These Markets

### The Opportunity
- Workers with AI skills command **up to 56% wage premiums** (OECD 2025)
- **39% of core workforce skills** will change by 2030 (WEF)
- 63% of young people already use AI tools, but few use them *well*
- Employers across all sectors now expect AI competencies — but almost nobody is teaching practical AI fluency at the "power user" level (between casual ChatGPT use and engineering)

### Why Armenia, Moldova, Georgia
- **Armenia:** Tech sector at 7% of GDP, growing 20-25%/year, 58,700 tech workers. TUMO and FAST already teach teens — but there's a gap at the early-career level. Government has an AI strategy but no workforce bootcamp program.
- **Moldova:** Ranked 59th globally in AI adoption. Digital Transformation Strategy 2023-2030 in progress. EU accession driving demand for digital skills. 56% rural population = massive digital divide to bridge.
- **Georgia:** IT sector grew from 0.4% to 2.6% of GDP in 4 years. 24,100+ IT companies. Banks leading AI adoption, but broader workforce lags. Post-2022 tech talent influx created knowledge transfer opportunities.
- **All three:** Young populations eager for economic mobility, growing tech ecosystems, EU/Western alignment creating demand for modern skills, and an **absence of practical AI bootcamps** at this level.

## Format

| | |
|---|---|
| **Structure** | 4 sessions across 2 weekends (Saturday + Sunday, Saturday + Sunday) |
| **Hours** | 7 hours/day (10am-6pm with breaks and lunch), 28 total contact hours |
| **Between weekends** | 1 week gap for students to apply skills at work/school + complete a mini-assignment |
| **Audience** | 18-25 year olds: college students, recent grads, early-career professionals |
| **Cohort size** | 15-20 students (see market analysis below) |
| **Prerequisites** | Laptop with internet. No coding. Basic computer literacy. |
| **Output** | Digital portfolio + capstone mini-project + "AI Expert" completion certificate |

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of the bootcamp, students will:

1. **Understand how AI works** conceptually — enough to explain it to their boss, professor, or team
2. **Prompt effectively** across multiple platforms, using structured methods and iterative refinement
3. **Spot AI failures** — hallucinations, bias, confidently wrong output — and know how to verify
4. **Build real workflows** combining AI tools to automate research, writing, data, content, and communication tasks
5. **Create portfolio-quality work** — writing, visuals, presentations, prototypes — using AI as a force multiplier
6. **Position themselves professionally** as the AI-capable person on any team
7. **Think critically** about AI ethics, job displacement, and responsible use in their specific cultural context

### SESSION 1 (Saturday): "Understand It & Command It"
**Theme:** Demystify AI + Master Prompt Engineering

**Morning Block (10:00-1:00)**

| Time | Activity | Format |
|------|----------|--------|
| 10:00 | **Welcome + "How Do You Already Use AI?"** — Live anonymous poll, share results. Set norms: this is about becoming a power user, not a programmer. | Full group |
| 10:30 | **"AI or Human?"** — Distinguish AI-generated vs. human text, images, code. Score yourselves. Discuss what's getting harder to tell apart. | Pairs -> group |
| 11:00 | **How AI Actually Works** — 25 min visual talk. LLMs as next-token predictors. Training data, tokens, probability, RLHF. No math — analogies and demos. Why AI is confidently wrong sometimes. | Interactive lecture |
| 11:30 | **Tool Showdown** — Same prompt to ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok. Compare outputs side by side. Which is best for what? When does it matter? | Hands-on, pairs |
| 12:00 | Break | |
| 12:15 | **The CRAFT Framework** — Structured prompting: Context, Role, Audience, Format, Tone. 3 worked examples with live improvement. | Interactive lecture |
| 12:45 | Lunch | |

**Afternoon Block (1:45-6:00)**

| Time | Activity | Format |
|------|----------|--------|
| 1:45 | **Prompt Olympics** — Timed team challenges: best cover letter, best meeting summary, best product description, best email to a difficult client. Peer-judged. Prize for winners. | Competition, teams of 3 |
| 2:30 | **Chain-of-Thought & Iteration Lab** — Break complex tasks into steps. Refine a prompt 5 times, document each iteration. Learn that prompting is a conversation. | Individual |
| 3:15 | Break | |
| 3:30 | **"Train a Classifier"** — No-code ML with Google Teachable Machine. See how models learn from data. Break them intentionally. Understand bias at the source. | Hands-on |
| 4:15 | **Bias Detective** — Prompt image generators for "a successful businessperson," "a doctor," "a teacher" in each of our 3 countries. What patterns emerge? Whose world did the training data reflect? | Group discussion |
| 5:00 | **Build Your Prompt Playbook** — Create a personal reference: 5-7 tested prompts for tasks relevant to YOUR work/school life. Document prompt, output, evaluation. | Individual |
| 5:30 | **Day 1 Wrap + Week Assignment** | Full group |

**Week Assignment:** Use AI intentionally for 3 real tasks at work or school this week. Document: what you tried, what worked, what didn't, what you'd do differently. Bring your notes to Session 2.

**Tools:** ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok, Google Teachable Machine, DALL-E
**Ethics thread:** Invisible AI, training data bias, cultural representation gaps

## Capstone Mini-Project Options

### 1. Workflow Overhaul
Document a real workflow at your job/school. Redesign it with AI tools. Build at least one working automation. Present the before/after with estimated time savings.

### 2. "AI Expert" Starter Kit
Create a practical guide + 3 ready-to-use AI workflows for your team, department, or organization. Include: tool recommendations, prompt templates, a training outline for teaching your colleagues.

### 3. AI-Powered Side Project
Design and prototype a small service or product: AI-curated newsletter, social media management system, tutoring tool, content pipeline. Deliver: pitch, one working workflow, sample output.

### 4. The Explainer
Create educational content that teaches a complex topic to a specific audience using AI throughout. Could be a video script, interactive guide, presentation, or illustrated explainer. Must include process documentation.

## Market Analysis & Pricing

### Armenia (Yerevan-based, potential Gyumri expansion)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Target demographic** | University students (18-22) at AUA, YSU, NPUA + early-career tech workers (22-25) at Armenia's 1,253 tech companies |
| **Addressable market** | ~15,000 university students in STEM/business + ~58,700 tech workers. Realistic reach for a pilot: 100-200 interested, 15-20 per cohort |
| **Max cohort size** | **18 students** — Yerevan has good coworking spaces (TUMO, Impact Hub). Limited by instructor attention, not by demand |
| **Pricing** | **$150-200 USD per student** (~60,000-80,000 AMD). Reference: TUMO is free but government-funded; private tech workshops in Yerevan run $100-300; coding bootcamps charge $500-2,000. At $150 you're accessible to students, at $200 you signal quality |
| **Revenue per cohort** | $2,700-3,600 (18 students) |
| **Costs per cohort** | Venue: ~$200-400 (coworking space, 2 weekends). Instructor: $500-800. Materials/tools: $0 (all free tier). Marketing: $100-200 digital. **Total: ~$800-1,400** |

| **Notes** | Partner with AUA or FAST for credibility + access to student pipeline. Armenian tech community is tight-knit — word of mouth is powerful. Consider Armenian diaspora pricing for online cohorts ($300-400 from US/Europe) |

## Scaling Path

1. **Pilot (Month 1-2):** Run 1 cohort in one market. Validate the content, pricing, and demand.
2. **Iterate (Month 3):** Refine based on feedback. Build case studies from pilot graduates.
3. **Expand (Month 4-6):** Launch in all 3 markets. Add diaspora online tier.
4. **Train instructors (Month 6+):** Develop a "train the trainer" program so local instructors can run cohorts. This is how you scale without being in 3 countries personally.
5. **Corporate tier (Month 9+):** Offer private bootcamps for companies (15-20 employees). Charge $2,000-5,000 per session. This is where the real revenue is.

## What Makes This Different From Existing Programs

1. **Speed:** 2 weekends, not a semester. Designed for busy people.
2. **Practical, not academic:** No theory exams. Everything is "can you do this at work on Monday?"
3. **Tool-agnostic:** Students use ChatGPT AND Claude AND Gemini AND Grok. The skill is prompting, not one interface.
4. **Career positioning, not just literacy:** The explicit goal is "become the AI expert on your team."
5. **Regional relevance:** Examples, exercises, and career strategies contextualized for Armenian/Moldovan/Georgian labor markets.
6. **Portfolio output:** Students leave with proof of what they can do, not just a certificate.
7. **Mid-week application gap:** The week between weekends forces real-world practice, which is where the learning actually sticks.
8. **Ethics woven in:** Every session has an ethics thread. No standalone "ethics day."
